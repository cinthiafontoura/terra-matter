About

The name TERRA MATTER comes from the Latin Terra Mater, which means Mother Earth, and we think that nothing matters more than our home, Mother Earth.

We are here to show you that we can save the Earth without leaving home, with small changes in our routines, living more consciously with the impact of small habits can change the environment.

According to the SEAI, on average, a home loses 20 to 30% of its heat through its walls and up to 30% through a poorly insulated attic.

Lights

Switch to LED lights. If you need electric lighting when working from home, switch to LED lights. They use about a fifth of the energy of halogen bulbs. They may be more expensive than halogen but will save you money in the long term. According to the SEAI, switching a single 42-watt halogen bulb to a 9-watt LED, will save about €7 a year. And LEDs last up to 10 times longer. Where possible, make the most of natural light throughout the day.

TV

Plug out all appliances when not in use. Pay particular attention to computers and printers. Even in standby mode devices from TVs to toasters are using 20% of the energy they would consume if they were switched on.

Kettle

Don’t fill the kettle. If you’re stopping work for a coffee break, only boil the kettle with as much water as you need. It’ll boil quicker and use less energy. A good rule of thumb for appliances is: if it makes things hot, then it uses a lot of electricity.
